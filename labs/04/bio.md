### Biography of Grace Hopper

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was a pioneering American computer scientist and a Rear Admiral in the United States Navy.  
She was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I and invented the first compiler, to translates English-like instructions into machine code.  
Her revolutionary work paved the way for the development of COBOL.  
She is also famously credited with popularizing the term "debugging" after finding an actual moth stuck inside a computer relay.
